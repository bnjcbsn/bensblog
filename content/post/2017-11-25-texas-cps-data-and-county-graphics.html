Exploring Texas CPS data, with maps

This exercise will begin by exploring confirmed CPS abuse/neglect allegations in Texas from 2008 to 2016. The data is publicly available on the open data portal of Texas.gov, specifically, CPS Abuse/Neglect Investigations Types of Abuse - Confirmed Allegations by County and Region FY08- FY16.

tok <- hidden::get_Texas_Socrata_token()

Get dataset…

dsLocation <- "https://data.texas.gov/resource/3pwr-c7hg.csv"
df <- read.socrata(dsLocation, app_token = tok)

My research diverges to a review of CRAN at the CRAN Task View: Analysis of Spatial Data. This led me to a recent paper by Robin Lovelace and James Cheshire found in the task view fourth paragraph, which further led me to Robin Lovelace’s Github repo for the paper. I elect to accomplish my own version of the plot in figure 7 on page 12 of the paper as this looks like an excellent milestone. First, I need to resolve my own data issue and narrow down the DPS figures to a single variable per Texas county. Then, as the Lovelace and Cheshire (2014) paper demonstrates, I’ll use dplyr to complete the join and tmap, a package first introduced to me in this paper. We’ll sum all allegations in 2016 per county and rename county to match the field in the spatial data. Then we’ll take a look at it before proceding with the join and plot.

confirmed_county_total <- df %>%
    filter(fiscal_year=="2016-01-01") %>%
    group_by(county) %>%
    summarize(confirmed_allegations=sum(confirmed_allegations, na.rm = TRUE)) %>%
    rename(NAME=county)
